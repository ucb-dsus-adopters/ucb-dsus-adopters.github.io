# Adapting Content

A major benefit of adopting is that you can **customize the materials for your
institution**, within the bounds of each course's license.

## Fork, then customize

When you fork a course's public student-materials repository, you get your own copy you can
change freely while still pulling updates from the original:

- Adjust datasets, examples, or context to fit your students
- Add or remove assignments to match your term length
- Keep your changes separate from the upstream materials

## Stay inside the license

What you may modify or redistribute depends on the license attached to each piece of
content. Read [Understanding Licenses](licenses.md) first, then check the license stated on
your course page:

- Content **without** an `ND` clause can be modified and redistributed (often with
  attribution and a non-commercial restriction).
- Content **with** an `ND` (NoDerivatives) clause, such as some textbooks, can be used
  as-is but **not** modified or redistributed.

!!! tip "Test before you assign"

    Run any adapted notebook through the autograder yourself before assigning it. Changing
    a question can change the expected output the autograder checks against.
