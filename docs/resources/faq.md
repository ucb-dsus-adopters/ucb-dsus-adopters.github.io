# FAQ

</div>

??? question "What does it cost to adopt?"

    The course and module materials and our support are free. Some optional tools have their
    own costs. For example, Gradescope is a paid service if you choose it for grading.

??? question "How long does adoption take?"

    Many instructors are running in **a couple of hours** once they have GitHub access and
    import the Canvas template. If a JupyterHub is being provisioned for you, that step can
    take longer depending on scheduling.

??? question "Do I have to use Canvas?"

    No. The notebooks are platform-independent and work with any LMS. Only the
    common-cartridge template import is Canvas-specific.

??? question "Do I need my own JupyterHub?"

    Not necessarily. You can use a campus hub, a partner-provisioned hub through
    [Cloudbank](https://www.cloudbank.org/) or [Cal-ICOR](https://www.cal-icor.org/), or
    alternatives like Google Colab or Binder. See
    [Infrastructure & Tools](../how-to-adopt/infrastructure.md).

??? question "Can I modify the materials?"

    Usually yes, though it depends on each course's license. Content without a NoDerivatives (ND)
    clause can be modified and redistributed. See
    [Understanding Licenses](../how-to-adopt/licenses.md) and your course's license section.

??? question "How does autograding work?"

    Assignments are Otter-graded notebooks. The easiest path is the web-based Otter Service
    Standalone; Gradescope and local grading are also supported. See
    [Otter Autograding](../how-to-adopt/autograding.md).

??? question "What do I do if I can't access the private solutions repo?"

    Confirm you accepted the GitHub invitations and that your username matches the form.
    See the troubleshooting steps on [Support](../how-to-adopt/support.md).

??? question "Can I get a hub without adopting a course or module?"

    Yes. [Cal-ICOR](https://www.cal-icor.org/) provisions computing infrastructure for
    California public colleges and universities
    ([calicor@berkeley.edu](mailto:calicor@berkeley.edu)), and
    [Cloudbank](https://www.cloudbank.org/) serves institutions nationwide
    ([Sean Morris](mailto:sean.smorris@berkeley.edu)). Neither requires you to adopt
    curriculum from this site. See
    [Infrastructure & Tools](../how-to-adopt/infrastructure.md).
